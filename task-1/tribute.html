Biography of Bob Marley:

  • Born in Nine Mile, Jamaica on 6 February 1945
  • Died 11 of May 1981 (aged 36) Miami, Florida, US
  • Was a Jamaican reggae singer, guitarist, and songwriter
  • Known as a Rastafarian icon, and he infused his music with a sense of spirituality
  • Began his professional musical career in 1963, after forming the group Teenagers with Peter Tosh and Bunny Wailer, which, after several name changes, would become the Wailers
  • The Wailers released its debut studio album The Wailing Wailers in 1965
  • Later on band's name became Bob Marley and the Wailers
  • Ranks as one of the best-selling music artists of all time, with estimated sales of more than 75 million records worldwide
  • Was posthumously honoured by Jamaica soon after his death with a designated Order of Merit by his nation
  • In 1994, he was posthumously inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame
  • He had mixed heritage, with African, European, and Syrian-Jewish ancestry
  • his father, Norval, was a Jamaican of English descent, while his mother, Cedella, was Afro-Jamaican
  • He married Alpharita Constantia "Rita" Anderson in 1966, with whom he had several children
  • He had several other children from different relationships, totaly had 11 childrens
  • His children have continued his musical legacy and have become influential figures in their own right

  • "None but ourselves can free our minds."
